Our mission

Dental emergencies don't follow business hours. A knocked-out tooth, a cracked crown, an abscess that won't stop throbbing at 2am — these are moments when you need fast, reliable information, not a search result that leads nowhere.

EmergencyDentistCA was built to fill a real gap: a California-focused resource that gives patients clear answers — which dentists are open right now, how much it will cost, and whether they accept Medi-Cal or walk-ins.

We are not a dental office. We do not provide dental care. We are a free patient resource that connects Californians to verified emergency dental providers across the state.

Who we serve

We built this resource for every Californian who has ever found themselves in dental pain and not known where to turn — adults, seniors, families, and students. We specifically focus on underserved patients: those without insurance, those on Medi-Cal, and those in mid-size cities like Fresno, Bakersfield, Stockton, and Modesto where emergency dental resources are harder to find.

Our Spanish-language pages are in development to serve California's 15 million Spanish-speaking residents, who are disproportionately underserved by existing dental referral resources.

How we verify providers

All providers listed on EmergencyDentistCA are sourced from public directories including Google, Yelp, Healthgrades, and individual practice websites. Ratings and review counts reflect publicly available data at the time of listing. We do not accept payment for placement in our standard listings — featured "Top Match" tags reflect affiliate relationships where we may receive compensation when patients call.

We recommend always calling a provider before visiting to confirm current hours, insurance acceptance, and availability.

Important: EmergencyDentistCA.com is an informational resource, not a dental provider. The information on this site is not medical advice. If you are experiencing a life-threatening emergency — including difficulty breathing or swallowing due to a dental infection — call 911 immediately. For all other dental emergencies, use our finder to locate a provider near you.
